"Remember

used to be?"

Odds are your site loads slower than a website from the 90s: let's fix that.

The promise of the internet is to make information instantly accessible to any person located anywhere on the planet.

Too often, web pages and apps are slow and clunky for anyone not using the latest iPhone with a 5G connection from a Western country. This makes for frustrated users and lost sales for businesses.

But obtaining consistently high global performance for complex applications is no simple matter. The tech giants have legions of engineers optimizing their apps, but how can startups with fewer resources achieve the same level of speed?

We believe that startups deserve an infrastructure platform that takes care of all the hard parts of global performance 'behind the scenes' so that they can focus their energies on their own products.

Specifically, a global infrastructure platform should:

  • free the developer from the nuisance of optimizations - you shouldn't need to manually tune a bloated webpack config file to get good global performance for your webapp; the platform should take care of that for you.
  • provide fast, rock-solid SLAs - developer's deserve a host that actually serves their content at the edge with consistently high performance 100% of the time, not the standard CDN deal from today that basically says: "we'll keep your content at the edge whenever we feel like it is convenient for us, and when it's inconvenient we'll just slow down your site without telling you."
  • abstract away the architectural complexity of muti-region applications so that developers can focus on writing the code that makes their business go, not messing around with convoluted architecture diagrams and wrangling cloudformation templates. Google 'aws multi-region deployment' to get a feel for how complicated things are today - we want to change that for the better.
  • provide trustworthy, transparent global monitoring - you should be able to measure how well your site is performing all over the world with just a few clicks. If the performance isn't where you want it to be, the platform should provide guidance to help you on your way.

Our mission is to build GlobeStack into a platform that does all these things and more. We're starting out tackling one aspect at a time, and we've decided to focus on the monitoring piece first - you can check out our first product, GlobeStack Insights, here.
